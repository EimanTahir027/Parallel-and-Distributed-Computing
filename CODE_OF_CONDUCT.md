# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and maintainers of this repository pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- Being respectful and considerate.
- Welcoming new contributors.
- Accepting constructive criticism gracefully.
- Focusing on what is best for the repository and learning environment.
- Showing empathy toward other community members.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, insults, or discriminatory language.
- Personal attacks or offensive comments.
- Sharing inappropriate or harmful content.
- Disruptive, rude, or intimidating behavior.
- Any action that makes the community unsafe or unwelcome.

## Our Responsibilities

Repository maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all repository-related spaces, including:

- Issues
- Pull requests
- Discussions
- Commit messages
- Code reviews
- Any communication related to this project

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the repository owner or maintainer.

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

## Enforcement Guidelines

### 1. Correction
**Impact:** Use of inappropriate language or behavior that is not harmful.

**Consequence:** A private warning with an explanation and a request to change the behavior.

### 2. Warning
**Impact:** A violation through a single incident or repeated behavior.

**Consequence:** A warning with consequences for continued behavior.

### 3. Temporary Ban
**Impact:** A serious violation of community standards.

**Consequence:** A temporary restriction from interacting with the repository.

### 4. Permanent Ban
**Impact:** A pattern of harassment or severe misconduct.

**Consequence:** Permanent removal from participation in the repository.
